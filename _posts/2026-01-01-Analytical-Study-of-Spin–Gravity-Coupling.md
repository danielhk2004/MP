This work investigates spin-gravity coupling in a plane gravitational wave spacetime by applying the Mathisson–Papapetrou–Dixon (MPD) equations. Using Rosen coordinates—chosen for their geometric simplicity—the study examines the MPD equations to first order in spin. The analysis explores how projecting the spin vector onto a parallel-transported orthonormal tetrad reveals conserved quantities, and how spin-curvature coupling leads to deviations from geodesic motion. The approach does not rely on a specific wave profile or initial conditions, allowing for a general treatment of plane gravitational waves with arbitrary polarization.
